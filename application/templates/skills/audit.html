Awareness Working Practitioner Specialist
Staff will have the digital skills and awareness they need to do their jobs effectively, as well as an understanding of the benefits of digital services in government (and the need to provide support to anyone who needs help accessing these). Staff will understand why people use digital services in their personal life, and how online services can be more efficient, faster, more timely (24/7) and simpler. Staff will have ‘beyond basic’ digital skills, with the ability to use a range of software and digital tools that are specifically relevant to the individual’s role. Staff will be able to make, and take a leading role in ensuring that others make, the most of digital opportunities in relation service delivery and understand what professions need to do to adapt and improve government services. They will also stay abreast of digital developments to help identify challenges and opportunities. Staff possess a range of specific specialist skills directly related to the individual’s role such as information and service design (e.g. coding), publishing (e.g. content design), communications and analysis.
Skills demonstrated include: The ability to use a computer or other digital device to send and receive email, keep safe online, use search engines and browse the Internet and intranet, as well as to upload, download and access attachments and fill in an online form. Skills demonstrated include: Staff will be confident users of digital and can use tools with direct application to their role such as a range of software applications, social media, mobile technology and a range of digital tools/open source products. Skills demonstrated include: Staff can support others in attaining working level skills, and understand the benefits of digital by challenging current approaches to providing information and services. Can also lead and support the development of digital plans and strategies. Skills demonstrated include: Staff can design new, and redesign existing. digital services and make expert use of advanced digital tools relating to comms and policy. Furthermore, staff will lead and / or be a part of multi-disciplinary Agile teams. Staff will also produce and deliver digital products and assets to support information and service design (e.g. product managers, developers, technical architects, designers etc.)
